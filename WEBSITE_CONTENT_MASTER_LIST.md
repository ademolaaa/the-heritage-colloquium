# Website Content Master List & Strategy

This document outlines the complete content structure for **The Heritage Colloquium** website. Please use this as a checklist to gather all necessary text, images, videos, and files.

## 1. Brand Identity & Global Settings
*Essential visual elements that define the site's look and feel.*

*   **Website Name**: (e.g., "The Heritage Colloquium" or "Ahiajoku Lecture Festival")
*   **Tagline**: A short, impactful phrase (e.g., "Where memory lives and the Future Begins").
*   **Logo**:
    *   **Primary Logo**: High-resolution PNG or SVG (Transparent background).
    *   **Favicon**: Small square icon for the browser tab.
*   **Colors**: Primary and Secondary brand colors (e.g., Gold, Black/Obsidian).
*   **Social Media Links**:
    *   Twitter/X URL
    *   Facebook URL
    *   Instagram URL
    *   LinkedIn URL
    *   YouTube Channel URL

## 2. Home Page
*The landing page that captures attention and directs traffic.*

*   **Hero Section (Top Banner)**:
    *   **Headline**: Main title (e.g., "Ahiajoku Lecture Festival 2025").
    *   **Sub-headline/Quote**: (e.g., "An intellectual harvest and banquet of ideas...").
    *   **Background Visual**: A high-quality **Image** (1920x1080px) or **Video** (MP4, silent background loop) representing the festival's grandeur.
    *   **Call to Action (CTA)**: Button text (e.g., "Download Programme", "Watch Live").
*   **Mandate Section**:
    *   **Title**: (e.g., "The Mandate").
    *   **Body Text**: 1-2 paragraphs explaining the core purpose of the colloquium.
    *   **Featured Image**: A cultural or historical image to accompany the text.
*   **Keynote Spotlight**:
    *   **Label**: (e.g., "2025 Distinguished Lecturer").
    *   **Speaker Name**: Full name and title.
    *   **Speaker Photo**: High-quality portrait.
    *   **Theme**: The title of their lecture.
*   **Community Feed Teaser**: (See Section 3).

## 3. Live Feed / Broadcast
*Real-time engagement for the hybrid audience.*

*   **Streaming Platform**: (e.g., YouTube Live, Facebook Live, Zoom).
*   **Stream URL**: Direct link to the live video or channel.
*   **Live Status**: Is the event currently live? (Yes/No toggle).
*   **Offline State**:
    *   **Offline Image**: A graphic to display when not live (e.g., "Tune in on Nov 25th at 10 AM").
    *   **Offline Message**: Text to display (e.g., "The broadcast has ended. Watch the recording in our Archive.").

## 4. About Us
*Deep dive into the organization's history and structure.*

*   **Mission Statement**: Full text describing the organization's goals.
*   **Vision Statement**: Full text describing the desired future impact.
*   **Core Values**: List of 3-5 values (e.g., "Integrity", "Culture", "Wisdom") with 1-sentence descriptions for each.
*   **Brief History**: Narrative of how and when the colloquium started (e.g., founded in 1979 by Dr. Sam Mbakwe).
*   **Leadership Team**:
    *   **For each member**:
        *   **Name**: Full name with titles.
        *   **Role**: (e.g., Director-General, Chairman).
        *   **Photo**: Professional headshot.
        *   **Bio**: (Optional) Short biography.

## 5. Lecture Series (The Archive)
*A digital library of past and present intellectual contributions.*

*   **For each Lecture (Past & Present)**:
    *   **Year**: (e.g., 1979, 2024).
    *   **Theme/Topic**: The central theme of that year.
    *   **Speaker Info**: Name and Title of the Guest Lecturer.
    *   **Lecture Title**: The specific title of the paper delivered.
    *   **Summary**: A brief abstract or description (2-3 sentences).
    *   **Visuals**: Photo of the speaker or event.
    *   **Files**:
        *   **PDF Download**: The full lecture paper (if available).
        *   **Video Recording**: Link to the full video on YouTube/Vimeo.

## 6. Gallery / Media Archive
*Visual documentation of events.*

*   **Collections (Albums)**:
    *   **Album Title**: (e.g., "2024 Cultural Night", "Inaugural Procession").
    *   **Year/Date**: When the event happened.
    *   **Description**: Brief context for the album.
*   **Media Content**:
    *   **Photos**: High-resolution images (JPG/PNG).
    *   **Videos**: Short clips or highlight reels (MP4).
    *   **Captions**: Description for specific key images.

## 7. Resources / Downloads
*Public repository for official documents.*

*   **Documents**:
    *   **Programmes**: Event brochures or running orders.
    *   **Blueprints**: Strategic documents or whitepapers.
    *   **Press Releases**: Official statements for the media.
    *   **Academic Papers**: Other scholarly works.
    *   **Format**: PDF is preferred for all documents.

## 8. Sponsors & Partners
*Recognition for supporters.*

*   **Sponsorship Tiers**:
    *   **Tier Names**: (e.g., Sovereign Patron, Heritage Guardian).
    *   **Benefits**: What sponsors get at each level.
*   **Current Sponsors**:
    *   **Logo**: High-res image of the sponsor's logo.
    *   **Name**: Company or Individual name.
    *   **Website**: Link to their corporate site (optional).

## 9. Blog / News / Press
*Updates and announcements.*

*   **Articles**:
    *   **Title**: Headline of the news item.
    *   **Date**: Publication date.
    *   **Author**: (e.g., "Secretariat", "Press Team").
    *   **Content**: Full text of the article.
    *   **Featured Image**: An image to represent the story.
    *   **Category**: (e.g., "Press Release", "Event Update", "Community").

## 10. "Ask Ahiajoku" (AI Chatbot)
*Interactive cultural guide.*

*   **Persona**: Define the AI's voice (e.g., "Wise Elder", "Academic Historian").
*   **Knowledge Base**:
    *   **FAQs**: Common questions and answers about the event.
    *   **History**: Key historical facts it should know.
    *   **Custom Instructions**: Any specific "do's and don'ts" for the AI (e.g., "Always greet with 'Nno'").

## 11. Contact Information
*How people can reach the secretariat.*

*   **Physical Address**: Office or Venue location.
*   **Phone Numbers**: Official contact lines.
*   **Email Addresses**: General inquiry, Press, Sponsorship emails.
*   **Contact Form**: Confirmation of fields needed (Name, Email, Subject, Message).
